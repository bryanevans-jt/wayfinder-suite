# Community Partners — Field Guide

**Employer outreach · Objection handling · Georgia**

Use this guide when visiting employers, making cold calls, or following up on Community Partners sign-ups in Wayfinder Pro. Employment Specialists and supervisors share this responsibility.

---

## The JTSG Mindset

When an employer hesitates, it is rarely because they lack empathy; it is usually because they lack information. Remember our platform: **The Golden Rule**. Treat their business concerns with respect.

1. **Do not argue.**
2. **Acknowledge and validate** their concern.
3. **Pivot** to the JTSG solution.

If you ever get an objection you do not know how to answer, say:

> “That is a fantastic question. Let me check with our team so I can give you the exact right answer. I will follow up with you tomorrow.”

Then note the question in Wayfinder Pro and follow up promptly.

## Before You Visit or Call

- [ ] Look up the employer in **Community Partners** — already listed?
- [ ] Prepare the **join link** from Wayfinder Pro (Community Partners → share join URL / form token)
- [ ] Know one or two participants who might fit their openings (optional)
- [ ] Review their industry — anticipate objections (retail vs warehouse vs office)

**Join URL (production):** `https://wayfinder-pro.thejoshuatree.org/community-partners/join`  
Use a tokenized link from the app when generating invites.

## Opening (30 Seconds)

> “Hi, I am **[name]** with **Joshua Tree Service Group**. We support people with disabilities in finding competitive employment. We maintain a **Community Partners** network — employers who want to hire ready, supported candidates. I would like to add **[company]** so our participants can apply through our system and we can stay in touch about openings.”

---

## Value Proposition (1 Minute)

> “For you: a single contact at JTSG, pre-screened applicants, and follow-up support after hire. For us: we track applications fairly and celebrate placements. Partners appear on our **map** so specialists across the state see you when matching jobs.”

---

## The Ask

> “Can I send you a **short online form** to join the network? It takes about five minutes — company name, contact, locations, and types of roles you hire for. You will be listed as a Community Partner — no fee.”

**If yes:** Send the join link via email or text on the spot.  
**If “not now”:** Leave materials + follow up in 30 days.  
**If “we only use our website”:** Ask if they will accept applications from JTSG-referred candidates; still log applications in Pro.

## Objection 1: “It Costs Too Much to Make Special Accommodations”

**Often heard from:** Small business owners, general managers watching the bottom line.

**Acknowledge:** “I completely understand keeping an eye on the bottom line, especially right now.”

**The pivot:** “The great news is that data from the Job Accommodation Network shows that nearly 50% of workplace accommodations cost absolutely nothing — like allowing a modified break schedule or a stool at a register. For the ones that do have a cost, the average is just $300.”

**The bonus:** “Plus, as a Partner, we will guide you on how to claim the Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC), which can actually put thousands of dollars back into your business just for making an inclusive hire.”

---

## Objection 2: “We Do Not Have the Time or Staff to Do Extra Training”

**Often heard from:** HR managers, shift supervisors, restaurant and retail managers.

**Acknowledge:** “Turnover is tough, and I know your management team is stretched incredibly thin.”

**The pivot:** “That is exactly why our Supported Employment program is so valuable. We do not just drop off a resume and wish you luck. When you hire one of our candidates, we provide the job coach. Our Employment Specialists will actually come on-site to help train the employee, assist with onboarding, and ensure they hit your productivity standards. We take the heavy lifting of training completely off your plate.”

---

## Objection 3: “Are There Safety or Liability Risks?”

**Often heard from:** Warehouse managers, construction, manufacturing.

**Acknowledge:** “Safety is absolutely the number one priority, and we respect how strictly you manage that here.”

**The pivot:** “Studies consistently show that employees with disabilities have equal, if not better, safety records than their peers because they are highly conscientious and follow protocols closely. More importantly, we do not guess. We only match candidates to roles we know they can perform safely.”

**The bonus:** “Depending on the candidate, we can sometimes set up a state-sponsored training period (WEX/OJT) where the state actually covers the candidate’s wages and workers’ comp for the first few weeks to completely remove your risk.”

---

## Objection 4: “We Are Not Hiring Right Now”

**Often heard from:** Everyone — this is the most common brush-off.

**Acknowledge:** “That is perfectly fine, and honestly, that is exactly why I dropped by today!”

**The pivot:** “The Community Employment Partners network is not a temp agency — we are building a long-term local talent pipeline. I would love to just get your company registered on our app today so we know what roles you typically hire for. That way, if a position opens up in three or six months, we might already have the perfect candidate trained and ready to step in. It takes two minutes, and it is completely free.”

---

## Quick Brush-Off Responses

### “I Am Too Busy to Talk Right Now”

> “I totally respect your time! I am going to leave this one-pager on your desk that explains the Partner Network. Can I shoot you a quick email next Tuesday to see what you think?”

### “You Will Have to Talk to Corporate”

> “Not a problem. I would love to leave you with this information, but do you happen to have the name or email of the best person at Corporate who handles local community recruiting?”

### “We Already Work With a Temp Agency”

> “That is great! We are not looking to replace them. We are a free, state-funded community partner. Adding us to your toolkit just gives you a secondary pipeline of highly dedicated local talent at zero cost to you.”

## Additional Objections (Short Responses)

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| “Send an email.” | “Absolutely — I will send the link now while we are talking.” |
| “HR has to approve.” | “Who is the best contact? I will send them the form and cc you.” |
| “We already work with VR.” | “Perfect — this complements VR; I am your JTSG specialist for **[region]**.” |
| “We had a bad experience before.” | “I am sorry to hear that. Our model includes on-site job coaching — would you be open to a conversation about what would make a partnership work for you?” |

## After the Conversation

1. Confirm the form was submitted (employer appears in Community Partners).
2. Log a **contact** on any related participant records.
3. If there is a hiring event, note it in the employer record for team visibility.
4. Set a follow-up reminder if they asked for time to decide.
